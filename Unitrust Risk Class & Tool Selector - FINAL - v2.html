Ok now I’m going to go over what the insurance does and what it doesn't and I’m going to start with the bad part first....

So, what the insurance WON’T Cover is Suicide, Death while committing a felony, and it won’t cover Death while participating in a riot.

So as long as you guys don’t plan on doing anything like that, you’re good....

What It Does Cover:

1. Death Benefit (Terms & FE)

So, with any insurance like this you have the death benefit...

This will cover any type of death – whether it's • Natural • an accident • or caused by pre-existing conditions...doesn't matter how, it will pay out within 24-48 hours

2. Terminal Illness (All Terms & FE Apps excluding Graded Policies)

This is where if you’re told by a doctor you have like 12 months or less to live as a life expectancy due to an illness or disease the insurance carrier will let you start using a portion of the death benefit while you’re living.

You can start paying the mortgage payment while you’re living. Most people usually have increased medical expenses and they usually can’t work during this time.

Critical Illness (Standard TERMS only)

If you’re able to qualify, you have a critical illness benefit, This is where if you have a heart attack, stroke, or cancer, you can start using a portion of the death benefit while you’re living based on the severity of the situation.

This is so you can use it for mortgage payments, loss of income, medical expenses – whatever you need to use it for.

Chronic Illness (Standard TERMS & Some FE Policies))

And the next living benefit covers Chronic Illness. This is where if you basically can't take care of yourself, for Example: :

• Bathing

• Dressing

• Eating

• Toileting

• Transferring yourself

• Or a severe cognitive impairment for 90 consecutive days

You can start using your death benefit ahead of time for the mortgage, your long-term care expenses, medical expenses, whatever you need to use it for its up to you.

1. WHAT’S THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN REGULAR LIFE INSURANCE AND THIS COVERAGE?

• The difference is old school regular life insurance just pays at death and that’s it…..you won’t see any of the benefit while living.

• Whereas this type of life insurance covers death and covers you while you’re living like we discussed earlier. This will allow you to access a portion of the benefit in cash while living. There’s no physical exam and today’s application process is simple and streamline.

…nothing happens – it goes wherever you go as long as you continue to pay for it. Your beneficiary(s) can still use the money for whatever they see fit as a tax-free benefit.
